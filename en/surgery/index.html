Surgery and Minimally Invasive Interventions

Modern surgical techniques and endoscopic operations without traditional incisions.

Minimally invasive and operative surgery — is a modern approach to performing operations with minimal damage to the patient's body tissues, which significantly reduces recovery time and lowers the risk of complications.

Thanks to the combination of surgical and endoscopic techniques (operative endoscopy), many procedures that previously required large incisions and long hospitalization are now performed through natural openings or small punctures under visual control.

Types of interventions:

  • Endoscopic polypectomy of the stomach, duodenum, and large intestine.
  • Endoscopic control of gastrointestinal bleeding.
  • Removal of foreign bodies from the GI tract.
  • Outpatient surgical interventions and minimally invasive treatment.

Advantages for the patient:

Minimally invasive surgeries have low pain levels, excellent cosmetic results (no scars), and allow the patient to return to full life within a few days after the procedure.
