Gastroscopy

Modern endoscopic examination of the stomach without fear, pain, and discomfort.

Gastroscopy (esophagogastroduodenoscopy or EGD) — is a highly precise method of visual examination of the esophagus, stomach, and duodenum using a modern flexible endoscope.

This procedure is the "gold standard" in diagnosing gastritis, peptic ulcer disease, reflux, and detecting early stages of oncological diseases of the gastrointestinal tract.

Indications for the procedure:

  • Pain or discomfort in the upper abdomen.
  • Frequent heartburn, sour belching, bitterness in the mouth.
  • Sensation of a "lump" when swallowing food.
  • Persistent nausea, vomiting, or rapid weight loss.

How to prepare?

The examination is performed strictly fasting. The last meal should be no later than 8–10 hours before the procedure. Clean water (non-carbonated) can be drunk in small amounts at least 3–4 hours before the examination.
